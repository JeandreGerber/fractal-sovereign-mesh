# The Founding Charter — Immutable Principles

These nine principles form the non-negotiable foundation of every node in the Fractal Sovereign Mesh network. A town that removes any of them exits the network and loses all partnership benefits: the shared defense fund, inter-corporate barter access, the energy and food mesh with cardinal neighbors, the Meta-AI coordination layer, and the global talent pool.

The freedom to leave is real and unconditional. So is its price.

**These principles are not open to modification proposals.** See [CONTRIBUTING.md](../CONTRIBUTING.md) for what is and is not open to change.

---

## Principle 1 — Free Energy

Every resident has the right to energy generated within the town's infrastructure.

This right cannot be revoked, priced, or used as leverage against a board member's voting behavior. Energy is infrastructure, not a product sold to residents. The town corporation produces it. The town corporation distributes it. No resident pays a utility bill.

*Implementation:* Solar arrays, wind systems, geothermal loops, and battery storage are corporate assets maintained by the West Node engineering discipline. Surplus energy is traded at the inter-node level, not charged to residents.

---

## Principle 2 — Free Food

The town's edible architecture and hydroponic systems provide baseline nutrition as a corporate utility.

No resident starves for lack of funds. The parks produce food. The buildings supplement it. The community food layer completes it. At full Pentad scale, the network's agricultural protocol covers 80-100% of caloric needs from within the network.

*Implementation:* Edible parks, rooftop greenhouses, hydroponic blocks, and kitchen gardens are distributed across the town. At single-node scale, the target is meaningful supplementation. At Pentad scale, the target is genuine food sovereignty.

---

## Principle 3 — Healthcare Access

Basic preventative and diagnostic healthcare is a standard corporate benefit, available to every resident regardless of individual income.

Mental health infrastructure — therapy, meditation, breathwork — is included. This is not charity. It is system maintenance. A resident in medical or psychological crisis is a governance liability and a security vulnerability. Prevention is cheaper and more humane than response.

*Implementation:* Med-Pod diagnostic systems, rotating care circles, trained peer supporters, and professional care resources at the clan scale (500+ residents) constitute the healthcare layer.

---

## Principle 4 — One Resident, One Vote

Voting power is a sovereign attribute of personhood in the town. It cannot be accumulated, sold, transferred, or weighted by equity size.

A billionaire founder and a resident who moved in last month have identical voting weight. The town corporation is not a traditional corporation where power follows capital. It is a sovereign community where power follows personhood.

*Implementation:* Each resident holds one cryptographic governance key issued on completion of onboarding. The key is non-transferable. Voting weight is hard-coded to 1 per key regardless of housing equity held.

---

## Principle 5 — No Permanent Power

No individual, circle, or role may hold the same governance position indefinitely.

Leadership is situational, task-bound, and temporary. The person who leads the hydroponic upgrade is not the same person who leads the legal defense response. Once the task resolves, they return to being a standard board member. Rotation is mandatory. Fixed power positions are the primary attack vector for regime capture.

*Implementation:* The AI identifies the most qualified resident for each governance task based on skills and history. Role tenure is capped. The AI enforces rotation and flags any individual accumulating disproportionate governance activity.

---

## Principle 6 — Radical Transparency

All AI decisions, treasury movements, board votes, and leadership actions are logged to the internal ledger and visible to every board member.

Governance in the dark is a charter violation. The AI's reasoning — why it chose a specific vendor, who it queried for advice, what factors drove a recommendation — is logged alongside the decision. No board member ever has to trust that the system is working correctly. They can verify it.

*Implementation:* The town's local blockchain ledger records every automated and human decision. The civic dashboard on every board member's phone shows the complete governance history in plain language.

---

## Principle 7 — Anti-Empire Clause

The town corporation may not retain ownership in any seeded town beyond the investment repayment date.

All seeded towns become sovereign peers upon repayment. No town may hold controlling interest in more than one other active town simultaneously. The network is a mesh of equal sovereigns, not a franchise empire with a parent company.

*Implementation:* Smart contracts govern the seed investment and automatically trigger ownership transfer upon repayment. The legal templates in `/node-formation/` enforce this structure in each jurisdiction.

---

## Principle 8 — Private Sovereignty of the Home

The town's monitoring infrastructure stops at the door of every residential unit.

No automated surveillance inside a home without a resident-triggered emergency signal — a fire detector activation, a medical alert, or a high-decibel distress signal above a defined threshold. The corporation owns the public space. The resident owns their private space. That line is hard and non-negotiable.

*Implementation:* All sensor systems are physically and architecturally prohibited from accessing residential interiors. Emergency override requires two independent trigger signals from resident-controlled devices.

---

## Principle 9 — We Are One Species

No node may operate policies that discriminate based on national origin, ethnicity, religion, cultural background, or any other immutable human characteristic.

The problems this network solves — energy poverty, food insecurity, predatory governance, monopolized healthcare — do not stop at borders. The solutions do not either. A town in Mexico and a town in Texas are equal partners. A node in Portugal and one in Kenya share the same protocols. The mesh is global or it is nothing.

*Implementation:* The HR screening process evaluates applicants on contribution capacity and philosophical alignment with these principles. It does not evaluate on demographic characteristics. Violations are charter violations subject to the exile protocol.

---

## On the Fork Mechanism

Any principle in the **plugin layer** — the town's product, governance structures, HR policies, investment strategy, physical design — can be modified by a 75% supermajority vote with a mandatory 180-day cooling-off period.

Removing or modifying any of the nine principles above requires exiting the network. The town retains the right to do so. The consequences are total and automatic.
